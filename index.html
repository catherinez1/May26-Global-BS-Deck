BS Key Movement Drivers — Global (G1)

Q3 FY26 (May 31) vs Q2 FY26 (Apr 30, 2026)
💵 Cash
▲ $3.8M (+2.3%)
  • Unrestricted cash increased $4.6M to $72.4M — Americas collections drove a $6.0M improvement, the primary operational contributor to the month's cash performance.
  • EMEA unrestricted declined $3.7M, driven by InterCard AG's merchant settlement cycle: the entity pre-funds merchant payouts from its own accounts before receiving card-scheme reimbursements, resulting in a temporary cash outflow. Analysis indicates this is linked to a concurrent AR build, consistent with the entity's acquirer/processor model. This pattern is operational and timing-driven, not a liquidity concern.
  • Restricted cash ($94.3M) is non-discretionary regulatory collateral held by EMEA payment-services entities and is not available for operational use.
📈 Net AR
▼ $9.4M — Active collections
  • Net AR declined $9.4M to $240.8M — a positive signal reflecting active collections in the Americas, which decreased $15.1M through normal monthly activity with no unusual items.
  • EMEA AR increased $8.7M, primarily from InterCard AG's card-scheme settlement receivables — a timing item that analysis indicates corresponds to the cash outflow reported in the same entity, consistent with the entity's merchant settlement model.
  • AR quality is stable; allowance held at $(12.0M). Overdue detail is available in the AR tab.
📊 ST Debt
▲ $8.1M — Corp Treasury
  • ST Debt increased $8.1M to $252.3M, reflecting the current portion of the Revolver and long-term debt. This is a Corp Treasury financing item; refinancing status and timeline are pending confirmation from Corp Treasury.
  • Excluding ST Debt, operational working capital is +$91.6M — positive and adequate. The reported WC deficit of ($160.7M) is entirely a function of this balance sheet classification.
  • Refinancing timeline to be confirmed with Corp Treasury.
📂 Accounts Payable
▼ $8.3M (–5.4%)
  • AP decreased $8.3M as the Q2 quarter-end elevation in April unwound naturally in May — a normal post-close normalization, not vendor pressure or early payment activity. No change in payment terms or supplier management.
  • EMEA is broadly stable. Brazil shows a minor increase reflecting local payment calendar timing, which is expected to normalize in the next period.
📦 Net Inventory (after PII)
▲ $7.4M MoM
  • Net inventory increased $7.4M to $125.2M, with build observed across North America, EMEA, and Petro segments. The underlying business drivers have been requested from Supply Chain Finance and are pending response.
  • The inventory reserve rate moved to 30.8% from 32.2% in April, as gross inventory grew faster than reserve additions. Reserve adequacy is pending confirmation from Supply Chain Finance.
  • SLOB aging analysis is pending for Q3 review. Reserve adequacy should be reconfirmed with Supply Chain Finance.
📑 Accrued Expenses
▲ $14.3M (+17.6%)
  • Accrued Expenses increased $14.3M — the largest single monthly movement on the balance sheet. The driver is a Term Loan interest accrual: a non-cash, auto-reversing accounting entry related to external debt obligations. This does not represent new cash expenditure.
  • The accrual is expected to reverse in the following period as part of the normal interest cycle. It has no impact on operational cash flow or underlying business performance.
  • Total asset decline of $22.6M is primarily driven by non-cash items: intangibles amortization ($11.7M) and FX-driven goodwill reduction ($4.6M).
